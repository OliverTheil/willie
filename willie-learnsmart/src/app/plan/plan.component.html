You only got a recommendation but you should still know the following.
The best way to learn things goes with the pomodoro technique.

It's a time management from Francesco Cirillo (1980) and stands for tomato. That's right! It's a tomato technique. Ok, no it isn't. It gets its name from a timer that breaks down tasks into smaller 25min-pieces. After a 25 minute task you get a 5 minute break. Also very common are tasks with a duration of 50 minutes and 10 minute breaks.
In a nutshell, after every little task of 25 or 50 minutes you get a break.

But within that time period, here's what you need to do:

- full concentration
- no smartphone! Airplane mode and put it away
- stay hydrated
- get snacks like some apple pieces or cookies
- optional: listen to something relaxing like lofi-beats
Explanation of the symbols:
50min

Learn 50 minutes, then a 10 minute break.

25min

Learn 25 minutes, then a 5 minute break.

Make a break of 10 minutes. Eat some snacks or make a coffee.

Repeat slowly 10 minutes your notes and don't hurry.

Optional: Repeat slowly 5 minutes your notes and don't hurry.
